Popular Places

Petronas Twin Towers

The iconic twin skyscrapers in Kuala Lumpur, once the tallest buildings in the world, featuring a sky bridge and observation deck.

Batu Caves

A limestone hill with caves and cave temples, home to a large statue of Lord Murugan and known for its colorful steps.

Langkawi

An archipelago of 99 islands with beautiful beaches, rainforests, and duty-free shopping, featuring the Sky Bridge.

Perhentian Islands

A tropical paradise with crystal-clear waters, ideal for snorkeling and diving with vibrant marine life.

George Town

A UNESCO World Heritage city known for its colonial architecture, street art, and diverse food scene.

Cameron Highlands

A cool hill station with tea plantations, strawberry farms, and hiking trails through lush greenery.

About Malaysia

Malaysia is a Southeast Asian country occupying parts of the Malay Peninsula and the island of Borneo. It's known for its beaches, rainforests and mix of Malay, Chinese, Indian and European cultural influences.

The capital Kuala Lumpur is home to colonial buildings, busy shopping districts such as Bukit Bintang and skyscrapers like the iconic Petronas Twin Towers. Its combo of colonial and contemporary architecture makes Malaysia a unique tourist destination.

With tropical climate all year round, Malaysia offers diverse attractions from the bustling metropolis of Kuala Lumpur to the pristine beaches and natural wonders.

There are many tourist attractions outside of the city, spread across Greater Kuala Lumpur and Klang Valley area which has hosted various major national and international events,

from man-made attractions to natural attractions such as mountainous landscapes, hills, and rice fields.

According to the Department of Survey and Mapping, Malaysia, there are 879 islands in the country.

The state of Sabah has the most islands with 395 islands within its waters.Apart from that, Malaysia also has 510 offshore geographical features which include rocks, sandbanks and ridges.

Malaysia has hundreds of islands. Some of them are habitable and passable; others are yet not explored and not habited. Malaysians refer to their islands as 'Pulau'

Most of the well known islands that can be found at Peninsular Malaysia are: Pulau Penang, Pulau Langkawi en Pulau Pangkor on the west side, and Pulau Perhentian, Pulau Redang and Pulau Tioman on the east side.

The islands on the west side aren't well known for their underwater world, i.e. you can't snorkel in many places.

These islands do have a lot of character in general and therefore are more crowded than the islands on the east side of the peninsula.

Islands on the east site are known for their great scuba diving and snorkeling possibilities.

Malaysia's culture is a vibrant mix of Malay, Chinese, Indian, and indigenous influences, reflecting its diverse population and history.

This multiculturalism is a defining characteristic, with various ethnic groups maintaining their distinct traditions, languages, and religions.

Malaysia a member of the commonwealth,represents the political marriage of territories that were formerly under british role.

When it was estsblshed on September 16, 1963,Malaysia comprised the territories of Malaysia(Now Penensular Malaysia),

the Malaysia island of Singapore,and the colonies of Sarwak snd Sabah in northen Borneo.

The long narrow,and rugged Malay Pensula extends to south and southwest from Myanmar and Thailand.

The soil of both portions of Malaysia have been exposed for a long period of time to intense tropical weathering,

with the result the most of their plants nutrients have been leached out.Soil tipically are strongly acidic and coarse-textured and have low amounts of organic matter.

The people of Malaysia are unevenly distributed betwwen Penensular and East Malaysia,with the vast majority living in Peninsular Malaysia.

The population show great ethnic,linguistic,cultural,and religions diversity. Within this diversity,a significant distinction is made for administrative purposes betwwen indigenuis people.
